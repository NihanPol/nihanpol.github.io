My research has focused on using pulsars to probe a range of physical phenomena acting on spatial scales spanning multiple orders of magnitude. Most notable of these has been exploiting the pulsar emission from one of the pulsars in the Double Pulsar (J0737-3039) system to measure, for the first time ever, the sense of rotation of a pulsar with respect to its orbit; modeling the double neutron star (DNS) population in the Milky Way to inform LIGO's search for DNS mergers; and using a Galaxy-scale pulsar timing array detector to search for low-frequency gravitational waves.

My current research involves:
- **Population modeling of binary neutron star (BNS) systems, especially relativistic BNS systems and BNS systems involving a black hole (stellar, intermediate and supermassive).**
- **Studying the Double Pulsar (J0737-3039) system and relativistic J1757-1854 system to perform tests of General Relativity in the strong-field regime and perform tests of fundamental physics in some of the most extreme environments found in the universe.**
- **Detect low-frequency gravitational waves using pulsar timing arrays with the North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves (NANOGrav) and the International Pulsar Timing Array (IPTA) collaborations.**
- **Fast radio bursts (FRBs): Study propagation effects as the emission from FRBs travels from the source, through its host galaxy and the intergalactic medium, to the Milky Way and Earth.**
